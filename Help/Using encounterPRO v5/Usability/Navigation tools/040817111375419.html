Description
The Office View is used extensively by the clinical staff and providers. The Office View serves two functions:

  • It serves as a gateway to all of encounterPRO's other screens and functions
  • It provides a visual presentation of the office and gives current patient status by tracking each patient from check-in to checkout

The Office view lists each room and station in the office. The rooms match the layout of your office and are defined when encounterPRO is installed.

How to Access This Screen

The OfficeView is the first screen displayed when you log in to encounterPRO. Use the tabs along the right side to display the various screens. Select Office to return to the Office View.

Screen Example



Office View Tabs
At the right side of the Office view (and also on all of the room screens) are several navigation tabs. These tabs allow you to access other parts of the patient record and to perform various functions. Select the link below to view an example of that screen.

  • Office - Displays the   Office View screen. This tab is a virtual representation of your office. It tells you how many patients are currently in the office, how long they have been waiting, and any tasks that are pending and/or in the process of being performed. The number of patients currently in the office appears to the right of the word Office. This tab allows you to return to the Office view from another screen.
  • Telephone - Displays the   Telephone Room tab. The number of patients currently in the telephone rooms appears to the right of the word Telephone. 
  • Chart Room/Holding - Displays the   Chart Room/Holding tab. The number of patients currently in the room appears to the right of the words Chart Room/Holding. The holding room is where encounters are kept until the doctor finishes charting. Certain workplans can be configured to send a patient to this room for review instead of proceeding directly to Checkout.

    NOTE: Billing information is not passed to the billing/scheduling system until a patient is checked out. Leaving a patient in the Chart or Holding room for an extended period will delay billing.

  • Waiting - Displays the   Waiting Room tab screen. The number of patients currently in the Waiting room appears to the right of the word Waiting. Clinical staff will use the Waiting room screen to monitor patients in the office, which provider they are waiting to see, and how long they have been waiting.
  • Checkout - Displays the   Checkout tab. The number of patients who have been checked out today appears to the right of the word Checkout. The Checkout screen is emptied automatically each night, you do not have to manually remove patients from Checkout. Checking out a patient triggers EncounterPRO to send the billing information to the billing/scheduling system. 
  • Charts - Displays the   Charts tab. Use this tab to search for and review a patient's chart when the patient is not in the office. 
  • Messages - Accesses the   Message List screen screen and allows you to read messages received and send messages to other staff members. (Internal Email System recommended for non patient related information).
  • Tasks - Accesses the   Task List screen that displays items assigned to you individually or to your role. You can review Tasks or create new Tasks for yourself or another staff member.
  • Utilities -  Accesses the   Utilities List screen screen. From this screen, you can perform such tasks as change your password, review outstanding labs and referrals, generate reports, and scan/post documents. 
  • Configuration  - Accesses the   Configuration List screen screen. Authorized users can perform many configuration functions from this list.
  • Logoff - Exits encounterPRO. Users should remember to logoff any time they leave their workstation.

What Are Those Numbers on the Tabs?

Tabs which have treatment rooms or can contain a list of patients will have a set of three numbers, such as 12/3/5. Each of those numbers indicates something about the patients and the waiting tasks (also called service bars) in that area.

What the numbers mean:

  • First number: the number of patients in that area or group of rooms
  • Second number: the number of tasks assigned specifically to the current user
  • Third number: the number of tasks assigned to the roles that belong to the current user
So, if the current user is Nurse Betty, and the Office tab reads 3/0/3 there are 3 patients in the exam rooms, zero tasks assigned specifically to Nurse Betty, and 3 additional tasks that are assigned to the roles that are assigned to Nurse Betty (such as Clinical Staff and Lab Nurse).

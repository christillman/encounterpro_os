How to: Close a Discontinued Medication.

Purpose
At each visit, the clinical staff needs to review the patient's medications and close any that are not current. Closing a medication indicates that the patient is no longer taking that medication. The ending date will appear in the patient's chart. Canceling a medication indicates that the medication was entered by mistake and should be removed from the patient's chart. If the patient was never taking the medication, you should cancel it. See How to: Cancel a Prescription Medication for instructions.

Details:

Steps
Follow the steps below:

  1. Access the Current Medications screen by doing one of the following:
    • From the Patient Chart, select the Meds box at the top of the screen
    • From the Enter Current Meds service bar
    • From the Meds box on the left side of the Vitals screen
    • As part of the workflow, the Current Medications screen appears automatically

    The open medications for this patient are listed. Medications that are not associated with a specific assessment are shown at the top of the list.
  2. Select the medication no longer being taken. A Treatment dashboard appears.
  3. Select the link at the top of the dashboard to close the medication (patient is no longer taking).
  4. Select Finished after Closing the medication to return to the Current Medications screen.
  5. Repeat steps 2 - 4 to close any other discontinued medications.
  6. Select Finished again to proceed in the workflow.

Description

This screen is used to help a user determine why encounterPRO calculated the E & M level for the category selected: History, Examination, or Decision Making. The rules encounterPRO follows for calculating E & M coding are displayed, with the levels reached for the category shown highlighted.

This is helpful when trying to determine why an encounter coded higher or lower than expected. If this information is not sufficient, then proceed to the Coding Rules and Encounter Data screen to see how encounterPRO calculated the E & M elements for a specific item/type.

IMPORTANT: EncounterPRO EHR calculates a suggested Evaluation and Management (E & M) visit level based upon the information documented during the encounter. The software measures the documentation recorded and applies configurations and algorithms to yield a value, but the software cannot evaluate the necessity of the work. Ultimately, the provider, not the EncounterPRO EHR, is responsible for determining the appropriate E & M level for each encounter. 

 

EncounterPRO EHR offers a suggested E & M level based upon the observations recorded and the configurations entered into the database.  The necessary configurations fall into categories that only the provider or the practice may provide (including the configurations that set risk and complexity values for assessments, as well as risk levels for treatments and progress notes), and configurations that are already in the master database that is initially provided to a practice (including configurations that associate histories with 'elements,' and associate physical examination results with 'bullets').  The reason that assessments and treatments are not pre-configured for the E & M coding wizard is that, under federal regulations, determinations of risk and complexity require the application of medical knowledge and judgment.  For the convenience of practices, the master database sets all assessment and treatment risk levels at 2 (on the scale of 1-4), and all complexity values at zero.  If a practice fails to adjust these values appropriately, the E & M coding wizard will produce wholly unreliable results.  On the other hand, if all the configuration values are set appropriately, providers who fully understand the encounterPRO EHR coding wizard and their own configuration choices may, over time, gain confidence that the visit levels suggested by the encounterPRO EHR are quite frequently appropriate (provided that the work performed was medically necessary).  However, the provider?not the encounterPRO EHR?must always choose (and be prepared to defend in an audit) the E & M coding level that is most appropriate.

How to Access This Screen
Access this screen by selecting:

  1. The number button for E & M Coding Level on the Patient Chart or Exit tab.
  2. Select the Details button below the category you want to review: History, Examination, or Decision Making.
  3. Select the Review Coding Rules button.

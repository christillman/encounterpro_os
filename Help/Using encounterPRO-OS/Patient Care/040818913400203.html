Description
Health Maintenance Rules alert providers when designated tests and procedures should be preformed for patients of a certain age or with a pre-disposing condition. The rules are set up for the entire practice and the system checks each patient against the HMRs to ensure that all routinely scheduled preventative care needed for that patient has been performed. The Patient Chart has a Maint tab that displays the HMRs that the individual patient qualifies for and the status of each.

Details:

Uses of HMRs
Examples of common health maintenance rules:

  • All patients over age 30 should have an annual physical exam
  • Women aged 18 and over should have an annual PAP smear
  • Men over age 50 should have an annual PSA test
  • All diabetics should have:
    • Hgba1c every 3 months
    • Ophthalmology referral every year
    • Urine for micro albumin every year, etc.


The lettering on the Maintenance tab on the Patient Chart screen provides an at-a-glance indicator of the patient's status. The lettering will be red if the patient is past due, yellow if a procedure is due within 30 days, or black if the patient is current.

Satisfying the HMR

Each HMR has one or more treatments that must be performed on a regular schedule to satisfy the requirements of that rule. Generally a list of procedures will be set up for the rule and if any procedure on the list is performed in the required time period that will satisfy the rule and the interval will reset.

If the procedure is performed outside the practice, it will need to be ordered in the Patient's Chart to update the HMR. You can either create an Entry of Records encounter and order the treatment as a past treatment (be sure to close the treatment), or order it as a past treatment at the next in-office encounter.

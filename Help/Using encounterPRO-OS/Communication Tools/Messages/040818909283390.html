Description
EncounterPRO incorporates an intra-office messaging system that allows you to send messages to any user or group of users in the practice. Messages are best used for person to person communication; use a Task if you want someone to perform a task.

There are two types of messages: patient-related and non-patient-related. Patient-related messages are associated with a specific patient and are saved in the patient's chart. The advantage of creating a patient-related message is that the recipient of the message can open the patient's chart directly from the message with a single touch. Non-patient related messages can be used to inform the office personnel of a staff meeting or change in office policy.

In office and Inbox Messages
When sending a patient-related message, it is important to note whether the patient is in the office or not in the office. Any patient who appears on the Office View is considered  'in the office'' This includes all patients in the Waiting room, Exam rooms, Telephone room, and Chart room. You will see buttons labeled for In Office Message and Inbox Message. Use Inbox Messages for patients not 'in the office.'

Messages can be assigned to either a specific user or to a user role, such as Provider. When you assign a message to a role, that message will appear on the messages list for every user assigned to that role.

Organizing Your Messages
EncounterPRO is delivered with three folders to organize your messages: Inbox (unread), Read, and Sent. When you read a message and mark it as finished, it moves automatically to the Read folder.
You can create additional folders as needed. If you create a new folder, you can place a copy of the message in the new  folder; however, this does not remove the message from the Read folder.

Messages in a Patient's Chart
The Messages /Tasks tab on the patient's chart displays the patient-related messages and Tasks for the displayed encounter. Scroll through previous encounters to review messages and Tasks relating to those encounters.

NOTE: Both messages and Tasks are considered legal documents and are never deleted. Patient-related messages and Tasks are permanently saved as part of the patient?s medical record. Also, keep in mind that messages and Tasks are not private. Certain users in your practice and all EHR support personnel have access to all messages and Tasks.

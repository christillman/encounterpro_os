How to: Link databases to Access tables Office 2003

Solution: Create a blank Access db and click 'Create'. Click 'File', select 'Get External Data', and click 'Link Tables'. Change 'Files of Type:' to 'ODBC Databases ()'. Click on 'New...'. Scroll to bottom of driver select box and choose 'SQL Server', then 'Next'. Name the file data source cpr_(version), click 'Next', and 'Finished'. Choose correct server from 'Server:' dropdown box. Click 'Next'. Click 'Next'. Check the box for 'Change the default database' to the name of your db and click 'Next'. Click 'Finished', then 'OK'.
Details: These step-by-step instructions were made using MS Office Access 2003 with SP1. Your mileage may vary. Resolution 1) Create a blank Access db (mdb) - name it cpr_(version level) and click 'Create'. 2) Click 'File', select 'Get External Data', and click 'Link Tables'. 3) At the bottom of the dialog box, change the 'Files of Type:' to 'ODBC Databases ()'. 4) Click on 'New...'. 5) Scroll all the way down to the bottom of the driver select box and choose 'SQL Server', then 'Next'. 6) Name the file data source cpr_(version), click 'Next', and 'Finished'. 7) Leave 'Description' blank, and choose the correct server from the 'Server:' dropdown box. For internal purposes, choose 'Techserv'. Click 'Next'. 8) Keep the defaults on the authenticity verification screen and click 'Next'. 9) Check the box for 'Change the default database' to the name of your db (eg cpr_(version) - for internal purposes, usually cpr_405_testing. Keep other defaults and click 'Next'. 10) Keep defaults on the next page and click 'Finished', then 'OK'. 11) Highlight again the 'Data Source' that you just created in the box and click 'OK'. 12) Check the 'Save Password' box, then click 'Select all', and then click 'OK'. 13) The 'Select Unique Identifier' box will pop up frequently - keep the tables linking by holding down the 'Enter' key on your keyboard until the last table opens on your screen (usually x_Translation_Set). 14) Close the table and you now have an Access db lined to the EncounterPro db of your choice.

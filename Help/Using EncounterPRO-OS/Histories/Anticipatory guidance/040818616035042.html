Description
Anticipatory Guidance, also called Health Education, is a type of history generally covered during well child visits. It allows the provider or clinical staff to document that they covered age-appropriate health and safety guidelines with the parent or guardian.

The format of the Anticipatory Guidance screen can be either drilldown or Yes/No, depending on your practice?s preferences.
Details:

Example
The Anticipatory Guidance for a 9 year old patient has listings for:

  • Seat belt safety
  • Bike helmet safety
  •  Cholesterol risk
  • Sun exposure
  • TB exposure
  • Firearm safety
  • Dental care
  • Adequate sleep
  • Regular exercise
  • Limiting TV and video viewing
  • Smoke detectors

The clinical staffer or provider will indicate which topics were discussed and can make comments for a specific item or a general comment.

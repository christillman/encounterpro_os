How to: Search for a Patient

Purpose
Many of the tasks performed by the office personnel begin by locating the chart for a patient. This is accomplished using the Charts tab of the Office View.

Before You Begin
You need to know the name and identifying information for the patient in question.

Details:

Steps
Follow the steps below:
There are several ways to search for a patient's chart:

  • Alphabetically by first or last name by selecting the ABC button and selecting letters
  • Typing a name or partial name
  • Typing a billing ID number
  • Typing in one of the other available search criteria

When you enter data in a field, press the Tab or Enter key on the keyboard or touch another field using your finger or the touchpen. The patient records matching your search criteria are displayed on the left side of the Charts screen.

If your search results do not include the patient you are searching for, try one of the other options, such as Date of Birth. If you still cannot find the patient's chart, select the Patient Status button to search the list of inactive patients.

The information displayed for each of the matching records will help you identify the correct patient when you have similarly named patients.

Select the colored patient bar to view the Patient Demographics screen. This allows you to view additional information to confirm that you have the correct patient.

NOTE: The permissions to perform all these tasks may or may not be granted to administrative personnel in your office, depending on practice preferences.

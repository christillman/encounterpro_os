Setting up an immunization dose schedule

Solution Title: Setting up an immunization dose schedule
Overview:

Listed below are all the tables that need to be 'lined up' to the immunization dose schedule and disease groups that display in the program

 

Each of the age_range.descriptions below need to be replaced with an age_range_id from c_age_range, which will probably need to be made by hand.  You may want to print this table out and hand-write in each of the age_range_ids that correspond, and the disease_ids for the diseases.

disease_id dose_number c_Age_Range.description last_dose_interval_amount last_dose_interval_unit_id sort_sequence dose_text
Diphtheria 1 6 Weeks - 18 Years   1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old.
Diphtheria 2 4 Months - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 4 months old and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Diphtheria 3 6 Months - 7 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 6 months old and less than 7 years old, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
Diphtheria 4 15 Months - 7 Years 6 MONTH 1 If the patient is at least 15 months old and less than 7 years old, then give the 4th dose at least 6 months after the 3rd dose.
Diphtheria 5 4 Years - 7 Years 6 MONTH 1 If the patient is over 4 years old but less than 7 years old, and their 4th dose was administered before they were 4 years old, then give the 5th dose at least 6 months after the 4th dose.
Hep A 1 18 Months - 18 Years     1 Give 1st dose anytime after 12 months old.
Hep A 2 18 Months - 18 Years 6 MONTH 2 Give 2nd dose anytime after a minimum of 6 months after 1st dose.
Hep B 1 0 Months - 18 Years   1 Give the 1st dose anytime after birth up to 18 years old.
Hep B 2 1 Month - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 1 month old and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Hep B 3 12 Months - 18 Years 12 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 9 months old and less than 18 years old, then give the 3rd dose at least 12 weeks after the 2nd dose.
HIB 1 6 Weeks - 5 Years     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old and less than 5 years old.
HIB 2 4 Months - 5 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 4 months old and less than 5 years old, then give the 2nd dose at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
HIB 2 12 Months - 5 Years 4 WEEK 2 If the patient is at between 12 months old and 5 years old, and the 1st dose was given between age 12 to 15 months then give the 2nd dose at least 8 weeks after the 1st dose.
HIB 3 12 Months - 5 Years 8 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 12 months old and less than 5 years old, and was less than 15 months when given the 2nd dose, then give the 4th dose 8 weeks after the 3rd dose.
HIB 4 12 Months - 5 Years 8 WEEK 2 If the patient is over 12 months old but less than 5 years old, and their 3rd dose was administered before they were 12 months old, then give the 4th dose at least 8 weeks after the 3rd dose.
HIB 3 6 Months - 12 Months 4 WEEK 2 If the patient is between 6 and 12 months old, and was less than 12 months old when given the 2nd dose, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
HPV 1 11 Years - 21 Years   1 Give the 1st dose to girls anytime after the patient is 11 years old.
HPV 2 11 Years - 21 Years 2 MONTHS 2 Give 2nd dose 2 months after the 1st dose.
HPV 3 11 Years - 21 Years 2 MONTHS 3 Give 3rd dose 2 months after 2nd dose.
Influenza 1 6 Months - 18 Years 12 MONTH   Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 months old. Can be given yearly for patients with certain risk factors.
Measles 1 12 Months +   1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 12 months old up to 18 years old.
Measles 2 4 Years - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 4 years old  and less than 18 years old, and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Meningococcal 1 11 Years - 21 Years     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 11 years old.
Mumps 1 12 Months +     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 12 months old up to 18 years old.
Mumps 2 4 Years - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 4 years old  and less than 18 years old, and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
pertussis 1 6 Weeks - 18 Years     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old and less than 7 years old.
pertussis 2 4 Months - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 4 months old and less than 7 years old then give the 2nd dose at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
pertussis 3 6 Months - 7 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 6 months old and less than 7 years old, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
pertussis 4 15 Months - 7 Years 6 MONTH 1 If the patient is at least 15 months old and less than 7 years old, then give the 4th dose at least 6 months after the 3rd dose.
pertussis 5 4 Years - 7 Years 6 MONTH 1 If the patient is over 4 years old but less than 7 years old, and their 4th dose was administered before they were 4 years old, then give the 5th dose at least 6 months after the 4th dose.
Pneu Valent 7 1 2 Months - 5 Years   1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old and less than 5 years old.
Pneu Valent 7 2 4 Months - 5 Years 4 WEEK 2 If the patient is at least 4 months old and less than 5 years old, then give the 2nd dose least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Pneu Valent 7 3 6 Months - 5 Years 4 WEEK 3 If the patient is between 6 months and 5 years old, and was less than 12 months old when given the 2nd dose, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
Pneu Valent 7 4 12 Months - 5 Years 8 WEEK 4 If the patient is over 12 months old but less than 5 years old, then give the 4th dose least 4 weeks after the 3rd dose.
Pneumococcus 1 2 Months - 5 Years     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old and less than 5 years old.
Pneumococcus 2 4 Months - 5 Years 4 WEEK 2 If the patient is at least 4 months old and less than 5 years old, then give the 2nd dose least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Pneumococcus 3 6 Months - 5 Years 4 WEEK 3 If the patient is between 6 months and 5 years old, and was less than 12 months old when given the 2nd dose, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
Pneumococcus 4 12 Months - 5 Years 8 WEEK 4 If the patient is over 12 months old but less than 5 years old, then give the 4th dose least 4 weeks after the 3rd dose.
Polio 1 6 Weeks - 18 Years     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old.
Polio 2 4 Months - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 4 months old and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Polio 3 6 Months - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 6 months old and less than 18 years old, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
Polio 4 4 Years - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 7 months old and less than 18 years old, then give the 4th dose at least 4 weeks after the 3rd dose.
Rotavirus 1 4 Months - 8 Months     1 Give 1st dose between 6 and 12 weeks. Do not start series after 12 weeks old.
Rotavirus 2 6 Months - 8 Months   1 Give 2nd dose at least 4 weeks after 1st dose but less than 32 weeks, Do not give any doses after 32 weeks old.
Rotavirus 3 9 Months - 18 Years     1 Give 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after 2nd dose but less than 32 weeks, Do not give any doses after 32 weeks old.
Rubella 1 12 Months +     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 12 months old up to 18 years old.
Rubella 2 4 Years - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 4 years old  and less than 18 years old, and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Tetanus 1 6 Weeks - 18 Years   1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 6 weeks old.
Tetanus 2 4 Months - 18 Years 4 WEEK 1 Give the 2nd dose after the patient is 4 months old and at least 4 weeks after the 1st dose.
Tetanus 3 6 Months - 7 Years 4 WEEK 1 If the patient is at least 6 months old and less than 7 years old, then give the 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after the 2nd dose.
Tetanus 3 7 Years - 18 Years 6 MONTH 2 If the patient is over 7 years old, then give the 3rd dose at least 6 months after the 2nd dose.
Tetanus 4 7 Years - 18 Years 10 YEAR 3 If the patient is over 7 years old but less than 18 years old, and their 3rd dose was administered before they were 7 years old, then give the 4th dose at least 10 years after the 3rd dose.
Tetanus 4 15 Months - 7 Years 6 MONTH 1 If the patient is at least 15 months old and less than 7 years old, then give the 4th dose at least 6 months after the 3rd dose.
Tetanus 4 7 Years - 11 Years 6 MONTH 2 If the patient is over 7 years old but less than 11 years old, and their 1st dose was administered before they were 12 months old, then give the 4th dose at least 6 months after the 3rd dose.
Tetanus 4 11 Years - 21 Years 5 YEAR 4 If the patient is over 11 years old and their 1st dose was administered after they were 12 months old, and the 3rd dose was administered before they were 7 years old, then give the 4th dose 5 years after the 3rd dose.
Tetanus 5 4 Years - 7 Years 6 MONTH 1 If the patient is over 4 years old but less than 7 years old, and their 4th dose was administered before they were 4 years old, then give the 5th dose at least 6 months after the 4th dose.
Varicella 1 12 Months +     1 Give the 1st dose anytime after the patient is 12 months old up to 18 years old.
Varicella 2 4 Years - 18 Years 3 MONTH 1 Give second dose at least 3 months after 1st dose.
